## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Deterministic scan:** `detect.mjs` over `app/page.tsx` + `components/` returned `[]` — **zero** findings. No gradient text, no side-stripe borders, no glassmorphism, no uppercase-tracked eyebrows, no headline overflow. Mechanically clean.

**LLM assessment:** It won't read as *broken-template* AI. But it IS the **saturated Stripe-minimal fintech reflex**: paper background, emerald (= money/savings) accent, Geist Sans/Mono superfamily, bento feature grid, mono numerals, a dark CTA band before the footer. Run the category-reflex check and it fails both altitudes — first-order ("subscription tracker" → clean light + green + mono numbers is the obvious guess) and second-order ("fintech that isn't navy-and-gold → emerald-on-paper Stripe-minimal with Geist"). It's tasteful and safe, which on a brand surface reads as generic, not refined.

**Visual overlays:** No live overlay injected this run — the CLI detector returned zero findings (nothing to highlight). Visual review was done from current CDP screenshots captured this session (EN/CS, desktop + 390px mobile; measured `scrollWidth == clientWidth`, no overflow).

## Overall Impression

The craft is genuinely high: responsive, localized (EN/CS with correct Czech ICU plurals), reduced-motion-aware, good focus states, and a *specific* product surface instead of a generic dashboard. But it's a well-made version of the default. You asked for something that stands out and isn't AI slop — it clears the slop bar on execution and the deterministic checks, yet lands squarely in the most predictable aesthetic lane for its category. The single biggest opportunity is a real point of view: a committed color/type strategy that a competitor couldn't have generated from the category name alone.

## What's Working

1. **The product surface as hero visual.** The mock (mono total, sparkline, upcoming renewals with monogram tiles + amber "renews soon" badge) is concrete and on-message — this is what lifts the page above text-only filler, and it doubles as the page's required imagery.
2. **System consistency.** One coherent token set, hairline borders, predictable spacing rhythm, mono reserved (mostly) for numbers. It feels engineered, not assembled.
3. **Real localization depth.** Locale-aware dates and grammatically-correct Czech plurals ("za 2 dny" vs "za 9 dní") are a level of polish most landing pages skip.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Muted text fails WCAG AA contrast
Several real-content tones fall below 4.5:1 on paper: `navy/45` = **3.0:1** (newsletter helper, mock "renews in", "Monthly spend" label), `navy/50` = **3.5:1** (hero stat labels, footer links, mock metadata), `navy/55` = **4.1:1** (footer tagline). This is the exact "muted gray on tinted near-white" failure the brief warns about.
**Fix:** floor body/secondary text at `navy/60` (4.84:1); push real paragraph text to `navy/70`. Reserve `navy/45–50` for non-text hairlines only.
**Command:** `/impeccable colorize` or `/impeccable polish`

### [P1] The aesthetic is the category reflex
Emerald-for-money + Geist + bento + mono numerals + paper is what an AI produces from "subscription tracker." No committed POV; distinctiveness is the job of a brand surface and this hedges.
**Fix:** pick a *named* strategy and commit — e.g. a Committed/Drenched palette (deep oxblood or near-black surface with one electric accent), or a characterful type voice. Carry the "money/control" idea through type + a signature device, not a green accent.
**Command:** `/impeccable bolder`

### [P2] Copy over-promises vs. how it works
Hero: "pulls every subscription… tracked automatically"; badge: "every renewal, tracked automatically"; mock: "synced just now"; stat: "1,200+ services detected." But How-it-works step 1 is "Add your subscriptions" manually. The page implies auto-detection the product doesn't do.
**Fix:** align copy with the manual-add reality (or commit to building detection). Drop/soften "detected/synced/automatically."
**Command:** `/impeccable clarify`

### [P2] Invented trust stats
"1,200+ services detected · 3 days avg early warning · $240 avg saved/year" are fabricated for a product with no users yet, and `$240` stays USD in Czech. Stress-testers and first-time visitors discount fabricated metrics.
**Fix:** replace with honest, verifiable value props (or remove the stat row until you have real numbers). Localize currency if kept.
**Command:** `/impeccable clarify`

### [P2] Repeated mono kickers = eyebrow scaffolding
"Why outlay", "How it works", "Pricing" — a mono-emerald kicker above every section heading. The detector didn't flag it (not uppercase-tracked), but it's the same AI section-grammar one tier over, and it compounds the brand register's "mono as "technical" costume" warning (mono eyebrows + mono footer line + mono "14 active").
**Fix:** vary the section cadence — drop the kickers, or make one a deliberate named system. Pull mono back to genuine numerics.
**Command:** `/impeccable typeset`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (First-Timer):** The hero says outlay "pulls" and "detects" subscriptions automatically, so Jordan expects a connect-your-bank flow — then How-it-works says type them in by hand. Expectation whiplash at the first scroll.

**Riley (Stress Tester):** Reads "1,200+ services detected" and "$240 avg saved/year," sees a brand-new product with placeholder footer links, and discounts the whole page as marketing fiction. Also: switches to Czech and the savings figure is still `$240`.

**Casey (Mobile):** Works well (no overflow, tap targets fine), but the two primary CTAs scroll off-screen fast and there's no persistent/thumb-zone action on mobile; the only always-reachable CTA is the small top-right "Začít."

## Minor Observations

- Dark CTA band before the footer is itself a SaaS convention; fine, but it's another familiar beat.
- Features are four bordered white cards on paper — bento mitigates the "identical card grid" trope, but it's still card-default; consider whether one feature deserves a non-card treatment.
- `$` currency isn't localized in Czech (minor, demo data).
- Newsletter placeholder switches to `vas@email.cz` in CS — nice touch, keep that energy elsewhere.

## Questions to Consider

- What would the *confident*, non-Stripe version look like — what's outlay's equivalent of "Liquid Death acid-green" that a competitor couldn't copy from the category?
- If the product is manual-add, what's the honest hero promise — "the subscriptions you forget, in one place" rather than "automatically detected"?
- Does "every recurring charge, always visible" want a visual metaphor (a calendar of charges, a runway, a countdown) stronger than a green accent?
